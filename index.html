Mechanisms

Scientific Principles

The core biological and engineering concepts behind PerfusionX.

Perfusion Dynamics

Flow uniformity — measured via pressure gradients and oxygen extraction — determines organ health during ex vivo maintenance. Adaptive adjustment of flow rate and oxygenation pushes circulation into weak regions and reduces overload in high-flow zones.

hemodynamics

LNP Transport & EPR Targeting

Lipid nanoparticles preferentially accumulate in regions of elevated vascular permeability — the Enhanced Permeability and Retention (EPR) effect. Stressed, inflamed tissue is intrinsically leakier, so LNPs concentrate exactly where intervention is needed without active guidance.

EPR effect

Inflammatory Signaling & DAMPs

Ischemia-reperfusion injury triggers cytokine cascades (IL-1β, TNF-α, NFκB) and releases damage-associated molecular patterns (DAMPs) that prime aggressive immune responses post-transplant. Reducing DAMP output during preservation lowers the immunological alarm that drives acute rejection.

cytokines · DAMPs

Closed-Loop Adaptive Regulation

Real-time sensor feedback — flow, pressure, O₂ saturation, metabolic markers — feeds a closed-loop control system that continuously adjusts perfusion parameters. This creates a self-correcting environment optimized for each unique organ.

closed-loop control

Horizon

Challenges & Future Applications

Current limitations and the frontier of next-generation transplant engineering.

Current Challenges

  • LNP formulation must be tuned carefully to minimize off-target deposition while maximizing uptake in stressed tissue.
  • Biological variability between donor organs means adaptive algorithms must generalize across diverse vascular anatomies and injury states.
  • Timing of LNP introduction must be precisely coordinated within the perfusion phase — too early or too late reduces therapeutic efficacy.

Future Applications

  • AI-controlled perfusion that autonomously optimizes parameters using machine learning on real-time biomarker streams.
  • Personalized LNP payloads tailored to donor/recipient immune profiles for maximum compatibility.
  • Extended viability windows enabling longer-distance organ matching and broader utilization of marginal donors.
